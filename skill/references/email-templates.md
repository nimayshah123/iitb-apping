# Email templates

Goal: a warm, specific, low-pressure cold email that's easy to reply to. One shared body, one **per-professor hook**, bold key terms.

## Subject line
`Prospective <Program> Intern (<dates>) — <focus> — <Name>, <Institute>`
e.g. `Prospective Research Intern (July–Aug 2026) — Machine Learning — <Your Name>, <Your Institute>`

## Base body (HTML — bold the key terms)
```html
<p>Dear Prof. <LastName>,</p>
<p>I am <strong><Name>, a <level> <degree> student at <Institute> (<key credential>)</strong>, currently applying for <strong><program></strong>.</p>
<p>My interests lie in <strong><field></strong>. <HOOK — one sentence tying their actual research to the applicant's interest.> Recently, I have worked on:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong><project 1></strong> — short detail</li>
  <li><strong><project 2></strong> — short detail</li>
  <li><strong><project 3></strong> — short detail</li>
</ul>
<p>I also <one line of additional credibility, e.g. an internship>.</p>
<p>Given this background, I was wondering whether there might be an opportunity to join your group through <program> during <dates>. I would be excited to contribute to any ongoing project that aligns with my interests, whether in person or remotely.</p>
<p>My CV and transcripts are attached. Thank you for your time and consideration.</p>
<p>Best regards,<br><strong><Name></strong><br><Institute><br><a href="<LINKEDIN_URL>">LinkedIn</a>&nbsp; |&nbsp; <a href="<GITHUB_URL>">GitHub</a></p>
```

**The signature MUST end with plain named hyperlinks** — display text `LinkedIn` / `GitHub`, href = the user's URLs. Use clean `<a>` links (NOT styled background "buttons" — they look spammy in webmail). Ask the user for both URLs if not in their profile; never omit them.

## Hook patterns
- "Your group's work on **<their topic>** maps directly onto my interest in **<applicant angle>**."
- "Your work on **<their topic>** is exactly the intersection of **<X and Y>** I am eager to explore."
- "Your work on **<their topic>** aligns closely with my hands-on **<applicant area>** projects."

## Follow-up (no reply after ~1 week)
Reply on the same thread, short:
```
Dear Prof. <LastName>,
I hope you are doing well. I am writing to gently follow up on my email from <date> regarding a possible research internship in your group through <program> this <dates>.
I remain very interested in your work on <topic>, and would be glad to contribute to any ongoing project.
If it would help, I am happy to share any additional information to assess fit. I would also be grateful to know whether your group might be able to host an intern for this period, and what the next steps could be.
Thank you again for your time and consideration.
Best regards,
<Name>
<Institute>
```

## Style rules
- Reorder the project bullets so the **most relevant-to-that-professor** item is first.
- Keep it ~150–200 words. No buzzword stuffing.
- Match the applicant's real credentials exactly (CPI, grad year, dates). Never inflate.
